A Note from Our President

Welcome to the Georgia Tech Interfraternity Council! As the president of IFC, it's my honor to represent an extraordinary group of fraternity men who are committed to the ideals of brotherhood, leadership, academics, and community service.

Our purpose is to govern and serve a united Greek community that is dedicated to amplifying students' potential, expanding networks, and improving the Georgia Tech experience. We are proud to represent a community of 30 member fraternities that each go above and beyond, both within and outside of Greek life.

Our men are leaders in many student organizations and active participants in athletics and research. Furthermore, over the past year our chapters have maintained an average GPA of a 3.58, consistently exceeding the total undergraduate average at Georgia Tech, as well as raising a total of over $220,000 for a multitude of philanthropic causes.

For prospective new members, Rush represents an exciting opportunity to explore what Greek life has to offer. Whether you're looking for a support network, a way to give back to the community, or an environment that challenges you to grow personally and professionally, I guarantee that there is a place for you here. The recruitment process gives students the opportunity to meet our chapters, learn about their values and achievements, and allow you find the brotherhood that will help you achieve your lifelong goals. I can easily say that joining Greek life has been one of the most positively impactful decisions of my college career, and I believe that everyone should at least give it a shot.

I encourage you to explore our website and learn more about what makes IFC at Georgia Tech so unique. If you have any questions or would like to connect further, please don't hesitate to reach out.

Go Jackets,

Tony Tanory
